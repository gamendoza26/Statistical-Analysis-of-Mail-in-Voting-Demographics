Data 1

Introduction and data

  • The data is from the American National Election Studies

    • American National Election Studies. 2021. ANES 2020 Time Series Study Full Release [dataset and documentation]. July 19, 2021 version. www.electionstudies.org

  • The data was gathered via interviews with 8,280 individuals pre-election and 7,449 individuals post-election. The data was gathered in a variety of interview formats, including web only, mixed web (web and phone), and mixed video (video, web, and phone).

  • We have written out a list of some key variables from the data. In parenthesis by each variable, we have written the code used in the data set to denote the variable:

    • Categorical Variables:

      • Sex (V201600), Sexual orientation (V201601), race/ethnicity (V201549x)

      • Voted in-person or by mail/absentee (V202117)

      • Party of US House Vote (V202111x), party of US Presidential vote (V202110x), party of Senate vote (V202112x), party of governor vote (V202113x)

    • Quantitative Variables:

      • Respondents were asked to give a thermometer rating between 0-100 about how they felt about certain groups after the election. Some thermometer ratings of note are those given to transgender people (V202172), gay men and lesbians (V202166), big business (V202163), police (V202171), scientists (V202173), journalists (V202175), Black Lives Matter (V202174), NRA (V202178), socialists (V202179), and rural Americans (V202184)

      • Data on the income level of the respondent has been redacted by ANES.

Research question

  • With this dataset, there is a wide variety of possible research questions. Some possible research questions include:

    • How is whether an individual voted by mail/absentee and their sex (categorical) associated with their “thermometer” rating of the NRA (quantitative)?

    • How is the party of an individual’s Presidential vote and their race (categorical) associated with their “thermometer” rating of Black Lives Matter (quantitative)?

  • For the first research topic, our hypothesis is that males who did not vote by mail are more likely to have a higher “thermometer” rating for the NRA. Conversely, we expect women who did vote by mail to have lower “thermometer” ratings for the NRA.

  • For the second research topic, our hypothesis is that Republican Caucasians are more likely to have a lower “thermometer” rating for BLM while Democratic people of color are more likely to have a higher “thermometer” rating for BLM.

Literature

  • Thompson, D. M., Wu, J. A., Yoder, J., & Hall, A. B. (2020). Universal vote-by-mail has no impact on partisan turnout or vote share. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 117(25), 14052–14056. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2007249117

  • The study analyzes states that implemented universal vote-by-mail, between 1996 and 2018. The study’s analysis counters a popular narrative in the media that voting my mail favors Democrats. The study found that universal vote-by-mail does not impact either political party’s share of turnout. Moreover, the study concluded that universal vote-by-mail did not increase the vote share of either party. Finally, the study found that universal vote-by-mail only caused modest increases in overall average turnout rates.

  • This study finds that universal voting by mail did not have partisan impacts in states that implemented it between 1996 and 2018. Our research will analyze whether voting by mail did have a partisan impact in the 2020 election - when voting by mail was implemented in the US at a larger scale than ever before because of COVID (by looking at highly-partisan thermometer ratings such as the NRA).

Literature

Literature

  • Find one published credible article on the topic you are interested in researching.

    1) Gondim, M. L. C., & Ferreira, J. (2012). O arco de desflorestamento na Amazônia: da pecuária à soja. Ambiente & Sociedade, 15(2), 23-40. https://doi.org/10.1590/S1414-753X2012000200002

  • 2) Marengo, J. A., & Nobre, C. A. (2001). Aquecimento global e mudança climática na Amazônia: Retroalimentação clima-vegetação e impactos nos recursos hídricos. Estudos avançados, 15(43), 37-52. https://doi.org/10.1590/S0103-40142001000300004

  • Provide a one-paragraph summary of the article.

    1st article: Brazil is the largest exporter and second largest producer of soybeans in the world. However, the increase in production has been directly associated to the deforestation of the Amazon Rainforest. This culture began in the South and moved toward the central region of the country, spreading gradually to its northern part. This growth was mainly based on monoculture properties controlled by large transnational corporations, and has replaced the local biome: the Amazon rainforest. Such fact has worried both public and private entities, since there has been an excessive deforestation and biodiversity loss. This study analyses the advance of soybean in the North of the Brazilian state of Mato Grosso, in the period 1984-2009, by means of satellite image interpretation. As a result, presents three maps of land use development in the area of study. It is noticed that the recent deforestation was due to the livestock activities and, afterwards, the eroded soil from the open fields and pastures were recovered with the soybean crops. In other words, in the analyzed area, soy plantations were not directly established over the deforested areas, but rather over areas previously degraded by livestock.

    2nd article: It discusses the complex relationship between climate change, vegetation, and water resources in the Amazon. The authors explore how climate feedback mechanisms, such as the water and energy cycles, are affected by deforestation, land use changes, and greenhouse gas emissions, leading to changes in precipitation patterns, temperature, and vegetation cover. These changes, in turn, have significant impacts on the hydrological cycle and water resources, affecting the Amazonian ecosystem and its inhabitants. The article concludes by highlighting the urgent need for better understanding of these feedback mechanisms and the implementation of strategies to mitigate the impacts of climate change in the Amazon region.

  • In 1-2 sentences, explain how your research question builds on / is different than the article you have cited.

    The first article cited provides insights into the relationship between deforestation and soybean cultivation in the Brazilian Amazon. The second cited article focuses on the complex relationship between climate change, vegetation, and water resources in the Amazon, while my research question focuses on the relationship between deforestation, carbon emissions, and burns in the Amazon. Thus, my research question builds on the article’s discussion of the impact of land use changes on the Amazon’s climate and vegetation by specifically examining the relationship between deforestation and carbon emissions in the region.
